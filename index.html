About Us

From Yangon to 710 Turk Street

Since the 80s, we’ve been cooking Burmese and Chinese food downtown as a family-owned business. We’re not your average Asian restaruant - we have Burmese food, which is basically food that takes three already well-known Asian cuisines - Chinese, Indian, and Thai - blends them in a mixer (not literally, because we’d be out of business if we did that) to create many flavorful dishes and unexpected dishes. Ever heard of coconut jello? The Burmese has that, and we sure have it on our dessert menu!

We still have orange chicken and fried rice if you want American Chinese food without going to Panda Express, and of course, Burmese food. Whether it’s picky children, long lines, or a long bus ride or drive that’s keeping you from trying Burmese food from Burma Superstar, Mandalay, or the other Burmese restaraunts that seem to be on every 2 corners of the Richmond, at least you got us, making food on a stovetop using whatever quality pots and pans we have.

Come downtown or leave work for lunch today to treat yourself (and/or your co-workers) here at Burma’s Den. We have chill vibes.
